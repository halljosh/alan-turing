ALAN TURING

A Brief History of Alan Turing

  • 1912 - Born in London, UK
  • 1931 - Begins studies in mathematics at King's College, Cambridge
  • 1936 - Theorizes a computational machine later known as a Turing Machine
  • 1938 - Begins work with British codebreaking organization GC&CS
  • 1939 - Develops Bombe, an electromechanical machine created to decode Enigma-enciphered messages
  • 1948 - Works on software for one of the earliest stored program computers.
  • 1949 - Theorizes a test of machine intelligence now known as a Turing Test
  • 1952 - Convicted of "gross indecency" (and chemically castrated) for a homosexual relationship
  • 1954 - Passes away due to likely self-inflicted cyanide poisoning
  • 2014 - Queen Elizabeth II retroactively pardons Alan Turing, leading eventually to the Alan Turing Laws
